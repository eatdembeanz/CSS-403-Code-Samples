Five Dialogue Writing Tips That Will Blow His Mind And Destroy His Penis

How does a solid piece of dialogue pan out? What can you do to keep a conversation flowing?

Hot Tips

  • Abusing pauses like commas, and ellipses... disrupts the flow of any sentence and makes it hard to keep the whole message in mind.
  • When a character is finished talking and a new character or event starts happening, it needs to be in a new paragraph.
  • It's a good idea to split a sentence of dialogue with a comma, so that you can intersperse "[character name] said" between it. This helps reiterate who is talking in this situation.
  • People don't often go on for more than two sentences without letting someone respond in a real conversation. Have your characters take turns speaking!
  • Avoid having dialogues run on too long without anyone taking action. While in real life people can spend hours just talking, in writing things can get very samey if two characters do it!

"We have to stop them!" Bob said. "They're going to crash!" Bob tried to pull the controls to the left, but Alice stopped him. "There's nothing we can do, don't get yourself killed!" she said.

We have to stop them," Bob exclaimed. "They're going to crash!" Bob tried to pull the controls to the left, but Alice stopped him. "There's nothing we can do, don't get yourself killed!" Alice said.
