Hot Tips for Writing Plots!

  • All setups really ought to have a payoff; why put a gun up in plain sight if it doesn't go off later?
  • Most plots follow the general structure of Exposition-Conflict-Climax-Resolution. Some stories don't establish a central conflict until very late in the story, and some don't even have anything that can be defined as a central conflict!
  • Don't sweat having your story fully plotted before you start writing. Many authors have only the ending and the beginning scenes decided before they start filling in the middle!

The plot starts with Bob deciding to go on a trip to the nearby town of Hillsburg. He loads his backpack with food and a gun, and sets out into the wild. As he leaves, he learns about the evil bandits on the road. He makes it to Hillsburg just fine, and the story ends with him going off to take a nap in a hotel.

The plot starts with Bob deciding to go on a trip to the nearby town of Hillsburg. He loads his backpack with food and a gun, and sets out into the wild. As he leaves, he learns about the evil bandits on the road. The bandits stop him, and steal his food! Using his gun, Bob threatens the bandits into giving up their life of crime, and takes his food back. He finally makes it to Hillsburg, and takes a well-deserved nap in a hotel.
